### Top 3 Recommendations

1. **Copy (C — 8/25):** Replace the five closing paragraphs starting with "This is really amazing stuff" through "Click the button below." They are pure filler with zero specificity. Rewrite with: (a) a specific outcome re-statement anchored to the 29% fasting glucose stat, (b) at least one testimonial or second proof element (e.g., number of users, before/after glucose readings), and (c) a single CTA with a concrete urgency mechanism (limited batch, price deadline, or quantity cap) instead of the generic "while supplies last."

2. **Brief (B — 12/25):** The ad's strategic angle — "your blood sugar problem isn't caused by what you eat" — is strong but abandoned after the mechanism section. Thread this angle into the close: explicitly contrast GlucoRestore with the failed dieting/willpower approach the reader has already tried. Add a price anchor or risk-reversal (guarantee) to make the offer concrete — currently there are no offer terms at all (no price, no guarantee, no bundle).

3. **Research (R — 14/25):** The opener "Did you know that blood sugar problems might not be caused by what you eat?" is awareness-appropriate but generic. Sharpen by naming the specific audience and their felt experience — e.g., replace with a scenario the ICP recognizes: "You cut the carbs, skipped dessert, and still watched your A1C climb at your last checkup." Use the audience's own language (A1C numbers, glucose monitor readings, specific medications like Metformin) to signal deep research and build immediate trust.
