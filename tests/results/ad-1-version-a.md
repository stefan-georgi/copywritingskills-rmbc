### Top 3 Recommendations

1. **Research (20/25):** Add a specific demographic or behavioral detail to the opening to sharpen the ICP — e.g., replace the general "when you look in the mirror these days" with a concrete scenario anchored in age or life stage ("If you're a woman over 45 who's spent hundreds on eye creams that promised everything and delivered nothing"). This grounds the reader immediately and filters the audience, improving both relevance and conversion.
2. **Brief (21/25):** Re-invoke the emotional driver at the CTA. The shame/embarrassment thread ("people started asking me if I was feeling okay") is powerful in the body but absent at the close. Before "Click below," add a brief future-pacing line that mirrors the opening emotion: "Imagine looking in that mirror and finally recognizing the woman looking back" — this closes the emotional loop opened in the first line.
3. **Copy (21/25):** Strengthen the CTA device itself. The arrow format ("→ Click below to claim your discounted bottle") is visually weak and generic. Replace with a benefit-loaded, button-style CTA that echoes the mechanism: "Get DermaThread Eye Restore — Rebuild Your DEJ for $49.95 (Save $40)" with a restatement of the guarantee directly beneath it. This makes the action more concrete and reinforces the mechanism at the moment of decision.
